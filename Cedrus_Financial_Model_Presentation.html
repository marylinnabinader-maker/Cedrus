A bottom-up, capacity-driven model — per-unit economics scaled by volume

Per-unit economics are built from cycle times and shared factory costs, then scaled by volume into a monthly P&L.

  • Capacity — cycle time, cavities & 23h × 26d run time set max units/machine
  • Volume = max capacity × utilisation % (revenue driver #1)
  • Unit cost — resin, fuel, depreciation, labour, rent, packing per unit
  • Profit — Revenue − COGS − OpEx − D&A − 17% tax
Three levers move everything: capacity utilisation, resin price, and selling price.

Where each dollar of cost comes from (COGS mix)

Resin + fuel drive ~87% of COGS — the model is highly sensitive to both.

Conclusions and watch-points

What the model says

  • Profitable from Year 1 — even at a conservative 40% utilisation: $221K net profit, $253K FCF
  • Strong operating leverage — 70% util. nearly triples FCF to $551K, payback ~2 years
  • Low break-even — EBIT turns positive at just 17.7% utilisation
  • Capex $1.2M recovered in 4.6 yrs (base) — faster as volumes ramp

Risks to manage

  • Resin price exposure — 71% of COGS; +30% wipes out most profit. Index customer pricing to resin
  • Selling-price discipline — a 10% discount halves EBITDA
  • Volume / off-take — economics hinge on filling capacity; secure committed demand
  • Fuel & FX — fuel 16% of COGS; LBP/USD rate (89,500) affects local costs
Bottom line: a robust, cash-generative project with healthy margins and a low break-even. Value creation is driven primarily by filling capacity and protecting spreads against resin inflation through indexed pricing.
